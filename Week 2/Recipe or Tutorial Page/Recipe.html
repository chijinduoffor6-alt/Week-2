Classic Nigerian Jollof Rice

Jollof rice is a beloved West African dish that is known for its vibrant flavors and rich history. It is a one-pot meal made with rice, tomatoes, onions, and a variety of spices. The dish is often served at celebrations and gatherings, and it has become a symbol of West African culture. In this recipe, we will show you how to make classic Nigerian jollof rice that is sure to impress your family and friends.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups long-grain parboiled rice
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 green bell pepper, chopped
  • 2 large tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)
  • 4 cups chicken broth or water
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the rice in cold water until the water runs clear. Set aside.
  2. In a large pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and garlic, and sauté until they are soft and translucent.
  3. Add the chopped red and green bell peppers to the pot and cook for another 5 minutes until they are tender.
  4. Stir in the chopped tomatoes and tomato paste, and cook for about 10 minutes until the tomatoes break down and form a thick sauce.
  5. Add the thyme, curry powder, paprika, cayenne pepper (if using), salt, and pepper to the pot. Stir well to combine all the spices with the tomato mixture.
  6. Pour in the chicken broth or water, and bring the mixture to a boil.
  7. Add the rinsed rice to the pot, reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for about 20-25 minutes, or until the rice is cooked and has absorbed all the liquid. Avoid stirring too much to prevent the rice from becoming mushy.
  8. Once the rice is cooked, fluff it with a fork and serve hot. Enjoy your delicious Nigerian jollof rice!

Finished! Enjoy your delicious Nigerian jollof rice!
